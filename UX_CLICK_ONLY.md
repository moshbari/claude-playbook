# Click-only UX

_Last updated: 2026-04-18_

## The rule

Every action in my consumer products must work with mouse clicks only. No keyboard shortcuts assumed. Even copy-paste should be a button, not Ctrl+C / Ctrl+V.

## Why

Same audience as the writing style rule — 35+ folks, often older, looking for a simple side hustle. The grandma-with-thick-glasses test. If she has to touch the keyboard for anything except typing her one idea, she quits. Webinar demos die the moment I have to say "now press Ctrl+C."

## How to apply

- Every action = a big labeled button. "Copy tags," "Copy title," "Download ZIP," "Open Etsy."
- One-click copy buttons on every piece of generated text.
- No dropdowns if a set of big tiles will work.
- No right-click menus. No keyboard shortcuts assumed.
- Pre-select sensible defaults. User clicks Generate and it just works.
- Typing the idea is the one keyboard step — keep it to that.

## Layout tips

- Big buttons. Minimum 44px tall on mobile, 48px on desktop.
- High-contrast labels. No gray text on light gray backgrounds.
- Obvious primary action per screen. One big button, not three equal ones.
- Success states that don't require reading — a green check mark is better than a paragraph.

## Don't

- No "click-to-edit" inline editing. Show an Edit button.
- No hover-only menus.
- No drag-and-drop as the only way to do something.
- No modals without a visible close button.
